1. Introduction 
IoT devices are everywhere — from smart sensors to environmental monitors — but one issue limits them all: battery life. Imagine deploying 1000 sensors only to find yourself changing batteries every few days. The problem? Inefficient power management.

You’ll learn official methods from Espressif and Arduino, use open-source GitHub examples, and discover tools that help measure and reduce power draw — all to make your IoT devices truly sustainable.

2. Use Sleep Modes & Duty Cycling 
The most effective way to save power is to keep your device asleep most of the time. Both ESP32 and ESP8266 include several sleep modes (light, deep, and hibernation). In deep-sleep, the current draw drops from ~80 mA active to under 10 µA idle!
Code Example (ESP32, Arduino IDE):
esp_sleep_enable_timer_wakeup(10 * 60 * 1000000); // wake every 10 minutes
Serial.println("Going to sleep now");
esp_deep_sleep_start();
When the timer triggers, the chip wakes, transmits data, and sleeps again — ideal for battery-powered sensors.

3. Apply DVFS & Clock Gating 
Even when active, your MCU doesn’t always need full power. That’s where Dynamic Voltage and Frequency Scaling (DVFS) comes in. It allows your device to lower CPU frequency and voltage during low workloads.
Code Example (ESP32, Arduino IDE):
setCpuFrequencyMhz(80); // reduce from 240 MHz
WiFi.disconnect(true);
btStop();               // disable Bluetooth
Reducing CPU frequency cuts current proportionally. Similarly, clock gating turns off peripherals (like ADC, SPI) when idle.

4. Choose Low-Power Communication Protocols 
Wireless communication is the biggest battery drainer in IoT devices. Choosing efficient protocols makes a massive difference.
For short-range, use Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE); for long-range, LoRa or NB-IoT; and if using Wi-Fi (ESP32), enable modem-sleep to turn off the radio between packets.
Example (ESP32):
WiFi.mode(WIFI_MODEM_SLEEP);
WiFi.setSleep(true);
This reduces idle current from 70 mA to about 5 mA during standby.

5. Energy Harvesting & Smart Power Management 
To go beyond optimization and reach energy autonomy, use energy harvesting — converting solar, vibration, or RF energy into power. Pair that with intelligent power management to recharge batteries or supercapacitors.
A simple solar IoT circuit can power a node indefinitely:
* A 5 V mini solar panel
* TP4056 charge controller
* 18650 Li-ion battery
* Low-dropout regulator for stable 3.3 V

6. Tools for Measuring & Tuning Power 
Before optimizing, you need to measure. Tools like uCurrent Gold, Nordic Power Profiler Kit II, and Monsoon Power Monitor give microamp-level precision.
You can also use open-source monitoring libraries to measure runtime power usage directly from your board.
Code Example (Arduino):
#include <ArduinoLowPower.h>
LowPower.deepSleep(5000);
